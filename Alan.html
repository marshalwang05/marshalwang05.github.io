Holding the title of the largest flower on earth, the Rafflesia is a group of parasitic flowers. It attracts flies and other insects with a smell of rotten meat and is dubbed the “corpse flower.” All known species are endangered and are threatened to extinction by logging, habitat loss or even plant collection. To help you can donate to the WWF foundation. Some ways are to stop habitat loss in the area, or even use them as a tourist attraction and use the money earned to help the Rafflesia. - http://wwf.panda.org/knowledge_hub/teacher_resources/best_place_species/current_top_10/rafflesia.cfm

With a conservation status of critically endangered and its population decreasing, the Iberian lynx is currently the most endangered feline species. Though their main goal is to increase the lynx population, they may have to save rabbits, as their population faces epidemics and is decreasing over the years, and is also the Iberian lynxes main source of food. As construction of roads continues, a form of habitat loss, it’s splitting up the Iberian lynx’s area, causing frequent car accidents, and they even face illegal hunting. There has been attempted conservation of these precious felines for over 10 years, and you can help by supporting the captive breeding program or sponsorship to the Large Carnivore Initiative for Europe, LCIE for short. - http://wwf.panda.org/our_work/wildlife/profiles/mammals/iberian_lynx/

The sudden increase of cattle from a mere 5 to a near 2000, caused the destruction and trampling of the albatrosses nests and along with it, dogs, cats and even pigs wreaked havoc on their eggs. Fences have been made to separate their nesting areas from the other animas, and many of the animals have been taken off the island. - http://www.ourendangeredworld.com/species/birds-bats/amsterdam-albatross/
